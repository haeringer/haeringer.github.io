oVirt relaunch - The Open Virtualization Platform


ben - Fr 23 September 2011 - Data center, Management

Among open source full virtualization solutions, KVM is currently the most promising one. The kernel-based hypervisor is the backend of Red Hat's RHEV, which is positioned as a competitor of VMware vSphere (hypervisor ESX). Searching for a free open source solution to manage KVM, I recently stumpled upon oVirt, which is also also developed by Red Hat. It looked good in the first place, but when trying to gather more information about it, it all seemed a bit dusty and quiet around it.. no guides, no updates for long time, etc..

But now the project will be relaunched completely, there is a new website already with new information and the project will now be pushed by Cisco, IBM, Intel, NetApp, Red Hat, and SUSE who have formed the Open Virtualization Alliance. The relaunch will officially take place on November 1st-3rd on the Cisco main campus. Interesting, that Cisco is pushing KVM and oVirt now, because they've always been completely into VMware. Here you can view the topics of the event and the goals of the Open Virtualization Alliance.

Nice to know that oVirt is not dead and I'm looking forward to watching the progress of this promising platform!
